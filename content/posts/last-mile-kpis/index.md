# Measuring Performance in Off-Grid Sales Teams

## Field Intelligence: Executive Summary

*   Prioritizes system survival and trust in volatile environments like rural Myanmar.
*   Tracks the efficiency of a unified marketing, sales, and service department.
*   Converts various behaviors into a single performance score for off-grid teams.

## What KPIs are Important for Last-Mile & Crisis Economy?

These metrics prioritize system survival, trust, and behavioral shifts in volatile environments like rural Myanmar.

*   Cash Discipline (Cash-Down Ratio): Measures the shift from high-risk consignment to immediate liquidity.
    ◦ Formula: (Total Cash-Down Sales / Total Sales Volume) x 100.
*   Trust KPIs (Repayment Rate): Evaluates the health of the "dealer economy" where contracts are absent.
    ◦ Formula: (Total Debt Recovered without Legal Action / Total Informal Credit Extended) x 100.
*   Consistency (Sales Days per Month): Rewards steady effort over sporadic large volumes.
    ◦ Formula: Total number of active days where at least one sales activity/visit was recorded per month.
*   New Product Launch Penetration: Measures how quickly the team adopts new inventory.
    ◦ Formula: (Sales Volume of New Product / Total Sales Volume) x 100 (typically measured over the first 3–6 months).
*   Monsoon/Seasonal Push: Tracks resilience during weather-related slumps.
    ◦ Formula: (Sales Volume during Off-Season / Average Sales Volume in Peak Season) x 100.
*   Customer Survival Rate: Indicates if customers are choosing to "fight" and restart after losses.
    ◦ Formula: (Number of Repeat Customers post-Crisis / Total Customer Base pre-Crisis) x 100.

## How do Revenue Operations (RevOps) KPIs Track Efficiency?

These track the efficiency of a unified marketing, sales, and service department.

*   Revenue Growth: The primary indicator of overall business expansion.
    ◦ Formula: ((Revenue in Current Period - Revenue in Previous Period) / Revenue in Previous Period) x 100.
*   Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC): Measures the investment required to gain a new user.
    ◦ Formula: Total Sales & Marketing Expenses / Number of New Customers Acquired.
*   Lifetime Value (LTV): Predicts total revenue from a single customer relationship.
    ◦ Formula: Average Purchase Value x Average Number of Purchases x Average Customer Lifespan.
*   Churn Rate: Measures the percentage of lost customers.
    ◦ Formula: (Customers Lost during Period / Total Customers at Start of Period) x 100.
*   Lead Conversion Rate: Efficiency of the sales funnel stages (MQL to SQL to Customer).
    ◦ Formula: (Total Qualified Leads or Customers / Total Leads Generated) x 100.
*   Marketing ROI: The return on investment for marketing spend.
    ◦ Formula: (Revenue from Marketing Leads - Marketing Cost) / Marketing Cost.
*   Lead Quality Score: Qualitative assessment turned into a metric to ensure alignment.
    ◦ Formula: A weighted average of lead attributes (e.g., budget, authority, need, timeline).

## What Qualitative & Strategic Management KPIs are Important?

These measure leadership, integrity, and organizational health.

*   Training Effectiveness (Capability Transfer): Measures if leadership is successfully building its "bench".
    ◦ Formula: (Number of Internal Promotions / Total Number of Management Openings) x 100.
*   Sales Team Integrity (Money Recovery Speed): Measures how fast "lost" or unremitted funds return to the office.
    ◦ Formula: Total Time (Hours/Days) from Discovery of Fraud/Gap to Full Fund Recovery.
*   Judgment (Bad Deal Refusal): Evaluates if managers are choosing "good" revenue over volume at any cost.
    ◦ Formula: Number of Potential Sales Rejected for Policy Non-Compliance / Total Leads.
*   Presence and Motivation: Measures engagement during holidays and crises.
    ◦ Formula: (Number of Sales Recorded on Public Holidays / Average Daily Sales) x 100.
*   The Triangle Framework Score: Assessing Mindset, Skillset, and Action during field coaching.
    ◦ Formula: Subjective scoring (1–10) by a manager across three pillars: Mindset (Resilience/Belief), Skillset (Competence), and Action (Effort).

## How do you Effectively Measure Through the Point System?

To make these KPIs actionable for off-grid teams, Sai Han Linn utilizes a Point-Based Matrix that converts various behaviors into a single performance score:

1.  Assign Weighting: For example, give 8 points for a cash-down sale but only 1 point for consignment.
2.  Tiered Multipliers: Use a multiplier that increases as total points increase (e.g., 500 MMK/point at low tiers; 1300 MMK/point at elite tiers) to reward high performers exponentially.
3.  Real-Time Visualization: Deploy Power BI or mobile app leaderboards so every rep can see their national ranking and earned commission in real-time, turning performance into a "sport".
