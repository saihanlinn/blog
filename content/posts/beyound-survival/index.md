# Beyond Survival: Building Resilient Businesses in Myanmar's Conflict Economy

## What Challenges Do Small Businesses Face?

Myanmar has faced decades of challenges, from political instability to ongoing civil wars. The 2021 military coup marked another turning point, exacerbating economic turmoil and severely disrupting daily life. Today, small businesses in Myanmar navigate an unprecedented landscape where skyrocketing logistics costs, inflation in basic food and medicine, and a lack of security create an environment that seems almost impossible to endure. Yet, amidst these challenges, many entrepreneurs continue to persevere with remarkable resilience and adaptability. Here are some of my personal working examples and how we all can adapt it.

### How Are Logistics and Supply Chains Impacted?

*   Unstable Logistics and Supply Chains: With 3/4 of the country deemed unsafe for travel, as per various advisories, transporting goods across regions has become a daunting task. Logistics prices have surged, putting immense pressure on business operations. These logistical challenges often result in delayed deliveries and increased spoilage of perishable goods, affecting the quality and reliability of services.

> **Field Data Evidence:** 3/4 of the country deemed unsafe for travel.

### What About the Costs of Essentials?

*   Skyrocketing Costs of Essentials: Basic goods like food and medicine have become unaffordable for many. Inflation continues to spiral, leaving businesses struggling to meet both operational and consumer needs.

### How Does Insecurity Affect Markets?

*   Insecurity and Fragmented Markets: The rise of multiple armed factions post-coup has destabilized many regions. The lack of safety hinders both local commerce and international investment.

## What Strategies Can Build Resilience?

1.  Focus on Essential Products and Services: Businesses should prioritize items with consistent demand, such as basic food supplies, hygiene products, or cost-effective solutions for daily needs. Leveraging locally available resources can reduce dependence on expensive imports and mitigate logistical challenges.

### What Can We Learn From Unilever's Shakti Initiative?

Example: Unilever’s Shakti Initiative The Shakti Initiative by Unilever in India serves as an inspiring example. Through this program, Unilever trained rural women as micro-entrepreneurs to distribute small, affordable packs of essential goods like soap and shampoo. These women, called Shakti Ammas, sold products door-to-door in underserved villages, improving access to hygiene products while generating income for themselves. This model highlights the power of focusing on essentials, leveraging local resources, and creating affordable product options to serve communities at the bottom of the pyramid.

2.  Build Resilient Supply Chains: Forming partnerships with local suppliers and communities can help stabilize supply chains. Cooperative efforts, such as resource sharing or bulk purchasing, can alleviate some of the cost burdens associated with logistics.

### How Can Bulk Purchasing Help?

Example: Bulk Purchasing in Agriculture As a consultant for several agricultural companies, I’ve seen firsthand how combining purchase orders from multiple agricultural input shops into a single route can significantly reduce logistics costs. Additionally, coordinating these routes to bring agricultural output back to client warehouses ensures efficiency and cost savings. This strategy not only minimizes transportation expenses but also strengthens the supply chain by fostering collaboration among businesses.

3.  Diversify Revenue Streams: Exploring alternative business opportunities, such as delivery services, repair work, or cross-border sales of local crafts, can provide additional income streams. Diversification is a key survival strategy in unpredictable times.

### How Can Environmental Services Companies Diversify?

Example: Environmental Services Company Diversification A top environmental services company I’m consulting for has successfully diversified into agriculture input by turning the food waste they collect into organic fertilizers. This strategy has been working well, allowing them to tap into a sustainable and growing market while still maintaining their core environmental business. The pivot not only supports waste reduction but also boosts their revenue streams through the sale of organic fertilizers.

4.  Access External Funding: Microfinance opportunities or aid from international NGOs can offer financial relief. Researching grants or programs designed to support businesses in conflict zones can provide much-needed resources.

### What Funding Programs Are Available?

Example: Yoma Bank’s "Grow Together BizPitch" Program Yoma Bank recently launched the "Grow Together BizPitch" Program to support micro, small, and medium enterprises (MSME) and startups in Myanmar. This initiative provides a platform for entrepreneurs to pitch their businesses for funding and support, helping MSMEs navigate the challenges of the current economy and achieve growth despite the difficult environment.

Example: Impact Hub Myanmar’s Access 2024 Impact Hub Myanmar is currently running Access 2024, a program designed to support Myanmar-based startups with funding and business development training. This initiative helps startups gain access to vital resources, mentorship, and funding to scale their businesses and thrive in challenging times.

5.  Innovate for Sustainability: Cost-cutting measures, such as reusing materials or exploring renewable energy options, can significantly reduce expenses while promoting long-term sustainability.

### How Can Businesses Streamline Operations?

Example: Business Group Streamlining Operations One of the top business groups I am currently working with has four different businesses, each with its own admin, marketing, and sales teams located in separate offices. Eventually, the company combined all the departments into one large shared team. They automated most HR functions and consolidated marketing, sales, business development, and customer services under one roof. This strategy not only streamlined operations but also improved efficiency by taking services from all the different companies, ensuring sustainability and operational cost reductions.

## What Is The Path Forward?

The challenges small businesses in Myanmar face are extraordinary, but so is the resilience of its people. The cultural resilience and strong community bonds in Myanmar offer a unique strength, supporting businesses through networks of mutual aid and local knowledge. In the face of economic and political uncertainty, adaptability, innovation, and community collaboration offer the best hope for survival. Entrepreneurs in Myanmar are not just surviving; they are setting examples of courage and creativity for others to follow.

By focusing on these strategies, small businesses can go beyond survival and start building a foundation for a brighter, more stable future. Resilience is not just about weathering the storm—it’s about finding ways to thrive despite it.
