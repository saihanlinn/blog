# Beyond Survival: Navigating Small Business Challenges in Myanmar

For small businesses in Myanmar, "business as usual" hasn't existed for a long time. The combination of unpredictable supply chains, fluctuating currency policies, and pervasive physical insecurity creates an environment that seems almost impossible to endure. Yet amidst these challenges, many entrepreneurs continue to persevere with remarkable resilience and adaptability. Here are some of my personal working examples and how we all can adapt it.

### The Realities of Last-Mile Collapse

The challenges are staggering. With 3/4 of the country deemed unsafe for travel, transporting goods has become a daunting task. Logistics prices have surged, putting immense pressure on operations and resulting in increased spoilage of perishable goods. Basic goods like food and medicine have become unaffordable, with spiraling inflation leaving businesses struggling. The rise of multiple armed factions post-coup has destabilized regions, hindering local commerce and investment.

### 1. Focus on Essential Products

Businesses should prioritize items with consistent demand, such as basic food supplies or hygiene products. Leveraging locally available resources reduces dependence on expensive imports. 

For example, Unilever’s Shakti Initiative trained rural women as micro-entrepreneurs to distribute affordable essential goods door-to-door in underserved villages, improving access while generating income. This highlights the power of focusing on essentials to serve communities at the bottom of the pyramid.

### 2. Build Resilient Supply Chains

Forming partnerships with local suppliers and communities helps stabilize broken chains. Cooperative efforts like resource sharing or bulk purchasing alleviate heavy logistics burdens. 

As a consultant for several agricultural companies, I have seen firsthand how combining purchase orders from multiple agricultural input shops into a single route significantly reduces logistics costs. Coordinating these routes to bring agricultural output back to client warehouses ensures efficiency and strengthens supply collaboration.

### 3. Diversify Revenue Streams

Exploring alternative business opportunities is a key survival strategy in unpredictable times. 

A top environmental services company I consult for has successfully diversified into agriculture input by turning the food waste they collect into organic fertilizers. This pivot taps into a sustainable market, maintaining their core business while boosting revenue streams.

### 4. Access External Funding

Researching programs designed to support businesses in conflict zones can provide much-needed relief. 

Yoma Bank recently launched the "Grow Together BizPitch" Program to support MSMEs and startups in Myanmar, providing a platform for entrepreneurs to pitch for structural funding. Similarly, Impact Hub Myanmar’s Access 2024 program supports startups with vital business development training.

### 5. Innovate for Sustainability

Cost-cutting measures like reusing materials or exploring renewable energy significantly reduce expenses while promoting long-term viability.

One of the top corporate groups I work with had four different businesses, each with separate admin, marketing, and sales offices. Eventually, they consolidated all departments into one large shared team. They automated most HR functions and streamlined operations under one roof, improving efficiency and ensuring operational cost reductions.

### Thriving Despite the Storm

The challenges small businesses in Myanmar face are extraordinary, but so is the resilience of its people. The cultural resilience and strong community bonds offer a unique strength, supporting businesses through networks of mutual aid. In the face of political uncertainty, adaptability and community collaboration offer the best hope. Entrepreneurs in Myanmar are not just surviving; they are setting examples of courage for others to follow. By focusing on these strategies, small businesses can go beyond survival.

### Frequently Asked Questions

**How does logistical instability affect small businesses in frontier markets?**
When regions become physically unsafe for travel, logistical costs surge and transit times become unpredictable. This unpredictability creates massive bottlenecks, severely impacting small businesses through increased transportation overhead and higher rates of spoilage for perishable inventory.

**Why is focusing on essential goods a survival strategy during economic crises?**
During high inflation and market volatility, discretionary spending plummets. Pivoting focus toward essential goods—such as staple foods, medicines, and basic hygiene products produced locally—ensures consistent demand and reduces a business's reliance on expensive, fragile import pipelines.

**How can competing rural businesses survive a broken supply chain?**
Through localized cooperation. Businesses can significantly reduce their overhead by combining purchase orders or sharing transport routes with neighboring enterprises. This bulk logistics approach alleviates individual financial burdens and builds a more centralized, reliable supply network.
