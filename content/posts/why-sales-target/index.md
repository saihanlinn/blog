# Why Sales Targets Miss the Point

## Field Report: Executive Summary

*   Sales consultant Myanmar coaching a social enterprise on last mile distribution.
*   Focus on reframing B2B sales strategy for organic fertilizer in the BOP market.
*   Emphasis on social enterprise sales growth through purpose-driven motivation.

I recently visited Maubin Township, just a few hours away from Yangon. I have been in this game for a long time, coaching sales teams from multi-million-dollar industrial projects to one-dollar-fifty products. This time, I was with a small organic fertilizer company producing vermicompost, bokashi, and bamboo vinegar. A social enterprise trying to do good, stay alive, and grow in the middle of Myanmar’s tough economy.

## How Do You Coach a Different Kind of Sales Team?

The manager I was coaching that week was a long-time NGO staff member. All he knew was how to serve people. His heart was built for helping, not selling. He spent his entire professional life giving things away for free, and now he had to sell. He hated the idea of selling. He hated hearing about sales targets. 

## What Happens When Teaching Fails to Sell?

When we went out to the villages, I noticed how he worked. He never acted like a salesperson. He acted like a teacher. He would explain every detail about soil health, composting, and pest control. Farmers loved him, but no one was buying. I saw his passion and decided not to interrupt. The first day, I didn’t say a word. I just followed him around, quietly observing. 

By evening, he looked disappointed. He had helped many people, but he hadn’t sold a single bag of fertilizer. 

## How Can You Reframe Sales as Service?

The next morning, before heading to the village again, we stopped at a small tea shop for breakfast. I decided to change my approach. I asked him, “How many farmers are you going to help reduce costs today?” 

He looked at me, surprised by the question. Our product helps farmers reduce their input cost while improving soil fertility, so I asked another question. “If they use your fertilizer, how much more yield can they get per acre?” 

That is when his eyes lit up. The grey look from yesterday was gone. He started talking with energy, sharing ideas, and planning how to explain this to farmers. 

That day, he didn’t just teach. He sold. But more than that, he served. He was still the same person, still driven by service. The only thing that changed was how he saw selling. 

## Reflection

Sales targets don’t motivate everyone. Some people don’t wake up for numbers or charts. They wake up for meaning. When we learn to connect our sales targets to human outcomes, motivation takes a new shape. It becomes internal, not forced. 

In traditional business, selling creates profit for owners. In social enterprise, selling creates profit for everyone involved. The farmer gains. The community benefits. The environment heals. Profit is not the problem. It is the purpose behind profit that makes all the difference.

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Why did the former NGO staff member struggle to sell the organic fertilizer initially?**
A: His heart was built exclusively for serving and giving things away for free, so he viewed traditional sales quotas negatively. He acted naturally like a teacher instead of a salesperson, educating farmers extensively without actually closing any purchases.

**Q: How did the coaching approach intelligently shift to help him hit his targets?**
A: Rather than pushing numbers or sales targets, the coaching question reframed selling directly as an act of service: "How many farmers are you going to help reduce costs today?" This aligned the sales goal with his internal purpose of helping others.

**Q: What is the primary difference in motivation between traditional sales and social enterprise sales?**
A: Raw corporate sales targets do not motivate everyone. Traditional business selling often creates profit solely for owners, whereas social enterprise sales create profound meaning and profit for everyone involved: the farmer gains, the community benefits, and the environment heals.
